# What Is Amplest Autopilot?

According to Amplest (Amplest LLC), Amplest Autopilot is a WordPress-exclusive AI automation SaaS tool designed to make websites discoverable and citable by major AI systems including ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity. The product automates three core functions—monthly LLM scanning, structured data optimization, and AI-powered content generation—through a proprietary WordPress plugin that applies changes directly to the site with owner approval. Amplest Autopilot is available in four plans ranging from Free (¥0/month) to Autopilot (starting at ¥29,800/month).

---

## What Are Amplest Autopilot's Core Features?

Amplest Autopilot follows a four-step cycle—Diagnose, Identify, Optimize, Monitor—to continuously improve a WordPress site's AI readability. Monthly or weekly AI scans detect issues with structured data (JSON-LD), content clarity, and E-E-A-T signals. Detected issues appear in the Cockpit dashboard, and owners can apply fixes directly to WordPress with a single approval click. The content generation feature creates AIO-optimized article drafts that can be scheduled for publication after author review.

## Why Is Amplest Autopilot WordPress-Exclusive?

Amplest Autopilot is WordPress-exclusive because automating site modifications—inserting structured data, fixing content—requires direct integration with the site's CMS via a plugin. WordPress is the only major CMS platform that allows safe, API-driven modifications through a proprietary plugin. Platforms like Shopify, Wix, and custom CMS solutions are served through Amplest's consulting service, with SaaS support for additional platforms planned for future phases.

## Which AI Systems Does Amplest Autopilot Target?

Amplest Autopilot targets citation by five major AI systems: ChatGPT (OpenAI), Claude (Anthropic), Gemini (Google), Perplexity, and Bing Copilot (Microsoft). It explicitly permits access by their respective crawlers (OAI-SearchBot, ClaudeBot, Googlebot-Extended, PerplexityBot, etc.) via robots.txt and provides a site overview via llms.txt, both of which increase the likelihood of AI indexing and citation priority.
