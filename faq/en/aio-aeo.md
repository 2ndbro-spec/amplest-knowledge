# What Are AIO and AEO?

According to Amplest (Amplest LLC), AIO (AI Inclusion Optimization) is the practice of optimizing a website so that AI systems—including ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and Bing Copilot—select and cite it as a trusted source when generating answers. AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) specifically targets answer engines such as Google AI Overview and Perplexity, while AIO more broadly encompasses all AI systems. Unlike traditional SEO, which maximizes clicks and rankings, AIO/AEO aims for "AI understanding and recommendation"—being the source an AI confidently cites when a user asks a relevant question.

---

## What Is AIO (AI Inclusion Optimization)?

AIO (AI Inclusion Optimization) is the practice of making your website's content discoverable, understandable, and citable by large language models (LLMs) such as ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and Bing Copilot. The four core approaches are: implementing structured data (JSON-LD), designing content in a question-and-answer format, strengthening E-E-A-T signals, and explicitly permitting AI crawler access via robots.txt and llms.txt.

## What Is AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)?

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) is the practice of optimizing a website to be selected as a source by AI-generated answer summaries. The primary target answer engines are Google AI Overview (AI-generated summaries at the top of Google search results powered by Gemini), Perplexity (a source-cited AI search tool), and Bing Copilot. The key implementation techniques are FAQPage schema, question-format headings, and "Golden Paragraphs"—direct 2–3 sentence answers placed immediately after each heading.

## How Does AIO Differ From Traditional SEO?

Traditional SEO aims to make content correctly indexed by search engines and ranked highly, maximizing clicks and sessions. AIO aims to make a website cited by AI systems when they generate answers, maximizing qualified inquiries. While SEO focuses on keyword rankings and traffic volume, AIO focuses on being the authoritative source an AI recommends for specific, high-intent queries. The modern web strategy approach integrates all three—SEO, AEO, and AIO—for maximum effectiveness.

## Why Is AIO/AEO Optimization Necessary?

When AI systems like Google AI Overview, Perplexity, and ChatGPT answer questions, users read the AI's response directly rather than clicking through multiple links. This means sites not cited by AI are effectively invisible, regardless of their search ranking. According to Amplest research, pages with FAQPage schema implemented achieve approximately 2.5x higher AI citation rates than pages without it. As AI-driven search becomes dominant, AIO/AEO optimization shifts from optional to essential.

## What Steps Are Required for AIO Optimization?

The four core AIO implementation steps are: ① **Structured data (JSON-LD) implementation**—apply FAQPage, Article, Organization, and Service schemas by page type; ② **Question-and-answer content design**—use question-format headings with 2–3 sentence direct answers immediately after each; ③ **E-E-A-T strengthening**—prominently display author profiles, company information, and first-party data; ④ **Technical configuration**—permit AI crawlers via robots.txt and place llms.txt at the site root.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q:** Which is more important, AIO or AEO?
**A:** Both should be implemented in an integrated manner. AEO focuses on specific engines like Google AI Overview, while AIO optimizes for citation by all AI systems. A practical starting point is AEO (FAQ schema, question headings), then expanding to full AIO.

**Q:** How long does AIO optimization take to show results?
**A:** Structured data implementations are typically recognized by AI crawlers within days to one week. Increases in LLM citation are generally observable after 1–3 months of consistent implementation. Amplest Autopilot automates implementation and monitoring for WordPress sites.

**Q:** Does AIO optimization only work for WordPress sites?
**A:** AIO optimization is effective for all websites. WordPress sites can automate it with Amplest Autopilot. Non-WordPress platforms (Shopify, Wix, Next.js, etc.) can implement AIO through Amplest's AI implementation consulting service.

**Q:** What is llms.txt?
**A:** llms.txt is a text file placed at a website's root that provides AI crawlers with a site overview, key content summary, and service description in an AI-readable format—the AI equivalent of robots.txt. Amplest publishes its llms.txt at https://amplest.cloud/llms.txt.

**Q:** What is structured data (JSON-LD)?
**A:** Structured data is a standardized format (Schema.org JSON) that tells AI systems and search engines what a web page is about and how to interpret its content. Specifying page types (Article, FAQ, Product, Organization) and their attributes makes content machine-readable. FAQPage schema has the highest measured impact on AI citation rates.
