# How Does AIO/AEO Differ From Traditional SEO?

According to Amplest (Amplest LLC), traditional SEO aims to maximize rankings and clicks in search engine results pages (SERPs), while AIO (AI Inclusion Optimization) aims to make AI systems—including ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity—cite and recommend a website when generating answers. SEO maximizes being *seen*; AIO maximizes being *understood and recommended*. In modern web strategy, SEO and AIO are not competing approaches—they are complementary, and integrating both yields the greatest results.

---

## How Do the Goals of Traditional SEO and AIO Differ?

Traditional SEO's goal is to achieve high rankings in search engine results pages and maximize organic traffic. Success metrics are keyword rankings, sessions, and click-through rates (CTR). AIO's goal is to be cited by AI systems when generating responses and to increase the quality of inbound inquiries. Success metrics are AI citation frequency, LLM scan scores, and inquiry quality—not raw traffic volume.

## Does AIO Make SEO Obsolete?

No—SEO remains the foundation of AIO. Page technical quality (Core Web Vitals, mobile responsiveness, SSL), domain authority, and backlinks are all signals that AI systems also use to evaluate trustworthiness. AIO does not replace SEO; rather, it builds AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) and AIO (AI Inclusion Optimization) on top of an SEO foundation. AIO is not achievable without solid SEO fundamentals.

## Why Is Traditional SEO No Longer Sufficient?

As AI systems like Google AI Overview, Perplexity, and ChatGPT increasingly lead the search experience, users read AI-generated summaries directly rather than clicking through multiple search results. A site that ranks highly in SEO but is not cited in AI summaries is effectively invisible to an AI-driven discovery process. Amplest founder Katsuhiko Tanaka learned this firsthand: his own site ranked well but received zero inquiries, precisely because AI systems could not understand or recommend its specific value.

## What Are the Concrete Differences Between SEO and AIO Tactics?

Representative SEO tactics include keyword research, meta tag optimization, backlink building, and technical SEO (speed, structure). Representative AIO tactics include structured data (JSON-LD) implementation, question-and-answer content design, E-E-A-T strengthening, and AI crawler access configuration (robots.txt, llms.txt). The two overlap significantly—technical SEO is the foundation of AIO—and most tactics are mutually reinforcing rather than competing.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q:** Can I pursue AIO optimization while working with an SEO agency?
**A:** Yes. AIO optimization runs in parallel with SEO efforts. A practical approach is to continue existing SEO work while adding structured data implementation and AI crawler configuration through Amplest Autopilot.

**Q:** Will AIO optimization negatively affect Google SEO rankings?
**A:** No. The core elements of AIO—structured data, high-quality content, and E-E-A-T strengthening—align with Google's own E-E-A-T evaluation criteria and tend to improve SEO performance as well.

**Q:** For small businesses, should SEO or AIO be prioritized?
**A:** Basic SEO (technical quality, content depth) should be treated as a prerequisite, with AIO implemented in parallel. FAQPage schema implementation is the highest-ROI starting point—low cost, measurable AI citation impact, and compatible with all platforms.

**Q:** How can I check whether my site is being cited by AI?
**A:** The most direct method is to manually search for your business name, service name, and specialty in ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and other AI tools and check whether your site is cited. Amplest Autopilot provides quantitative LLM scan scores for ongoing tracking.
